📋 Documents Needed After NGO Registration

Since your NGO is registered under the Indian Trusts Act 1882, here are the documents and registrations you must obtain:

URGENT

🏦 NGO / Trust PAN Card

Income Tax Dept → Apply on NSDL/UTIITSL

Essential for opening a bank account and receiving donations. Apply within 30 days.

URGENT

🏛️ NGO Bank Account

Any Nationalised Bank (SBI recommended)

Open in Trust's name with Trust Deed + PAN + Trustee IDs. Required for Razorpay.

IMPORTANT

📱 NGO Darpan Registration

ngo.india.gov.in → Free

Government's NGO portal. Required for grants & government schemes. Brings credibility.

IMPORTANT

🎁 Section 12A / 12AB Certificate

Income Tax Dept → Form 10A

Exempts NGO income from tax. Must apply within 3 months of registration.

IMPORTANT

💚 Section 80G Certification

Income Tax Dept → After 12A

Allows donors to claim 50% tax deduction. Critical for attracting donations.

OPTIONAL

🌍 FCRA Registration

Ministry of Home Affairs

Required only if you plan to receive foreign funds/donations. Apply after 3 years of operation.

LATER

📊 Annual Return Filing

Income Tax Dept → Form ITR-7

File annual income tax return every year by 31st Oct. Mandatory even if income is nil.

LATER

📰 MSME / DARPAN Udyam

udyamregistration.gov.in

Register as NGO on Udyam portal for government schemes and CSR funding eligibility.

📅 Action Timeline:
Week 1–2: Open Trust Bank Account + Apply for Trust PAN
Month 1: Register on NGO Darpan + Apply 12A/12AB (Form 10A online)
Month 2–3: Apply for 80G Certification + Set up Razorpay with NGO details
Month 6+: Apply Google for Nonprofits (free Google Workspace) + FCRA if needed
Annual: File ITR-7 + Audited accounts + Maintain receipt & expenditure register

💡 Tip: Your website already has donation + membership payment — once you get 80G, your Razorpay account gets auto-verified for NGO mode which reduces TDR fees.
